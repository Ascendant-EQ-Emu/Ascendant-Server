# Governance

## Purpose

Ascendant Server is intended to be a community-maintained project that can continue beyond any one operator, host, or steward.

This document describes how stewardship, contribution, and continuity are handled.

## Roles

### Community Stewards

Community Stewards are trusted project stewards who share responsibility for the long-term continuity and administration of the project.

Community Stewards are responsible for:

- repository administration
- continuity of the project
- governance decisions
- project preservation
- review and merge of pull requests
- oversight of community-controlled private materials, where applicable

For GitHub permission purposes, Community Stewards may hold organization owner permissions. That platform role exists to support continuity and administration, not personal ownership of the project.

### Contributors

Contributors are community members who propose changes, improvements, fixes, and additions through pull requests, issues, discussion, or documentation.

Contributors do not need special repository permissions in order to participate. Community contribution through forks and pull requests is encouraged.

## Decision Making

The project should be operated with a bias toward practical collaboration, continuity, and transparency.

In general:

- routine repository changes may be reviewed and merged by Community Stewards
- larger structural, governance, or continuity-related decisions should be discussed among the Community Stewards
- sensitive matters involving private data, access control, or operational continuity should be handled by the Community Stewards

Where practical, decisions should be made by rough consensus among the active Community Stewards.

## Continuity

Ascendant Server is intended to outlast any one operator or host.

If a founding maintainer or live operator steps away, the project may continue under the stewardship of the Community Stewards.

The public repository, governance, and project direction are intended to remain community-maintained even if a live Ascendant server instance changes operators or ceases operation.

## Live Server vs. Repository

The public repository is not the same thing as any live operated Ascendant server.

The repository is the community-maintained codebase and baseline project structure.

A live server is an operator-run deployment of that codebase and may include private data and operational materials that are not part of the public repository.

## Private Community-Controlled Materials

Some materials may be stewarded privately on behalf of the project, including:

- production database backups
- restore procedures
- access-control documentation
- sensitive operational continuity materials

These materials are not public by default. They may be community-controlled with restricted access and privacy protections in place.

## Community Steward Changes

Community Stewards may add additional Community Stewards as needed for project continuity and health.

A Community Steward may be removed if they:

- abuse access
- expose sensitive information improperly
- act against the long-term interests of the project
- become inactive for an extended period with no transition plan

Where practical, such decisions should be discussed among the active Community Stewards before action is taken.

## Scope of Governance

This governance model applies to the repository and community-maintained project structure.

It does not automatically grant access to private production systems, live player data, or private operational environments. Access to such materials should be granted intentionally and only where appropriate.

## Changes to This Document

This governance document may be revised over time as the project matures.
